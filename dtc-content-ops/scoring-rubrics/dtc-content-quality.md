# DTC Content Quality Rubric

**Total: 100 points across 4 dimensions (25 each)**
**Pass threshold: 90/100**
**Iteration: Content below 90 is revised and re-scored, max 3 iterations**

---

## Dimension 1: Problem-First Hook (25 points)

Does the content lead with the customer's pain point, not the product?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 25 | Opens with a specific, relatable problem. Reader recognizes their own struggle before encountering any product mention. |
| 20 | Opens with a general problem area but could be more specific. Product appears in first third but doesn't lead. |
| 15 | Problem is mentioned but product leads or co-leads. Reader encounters the product before fully feeling the pain. |
| 10 | Product-first with problem as afterthought. "Introducing X — for people who struggle with Y." |
| 5 | Pure product pitch. No problem framing at all. |
| 0 | No text content. |

**What to look for:**
- First sentence should describe a situation, not a product
- Questions that make the reader nod ("Ever notice how...")
- Specific scenarios over generic claims ("When you're 16 weeks out and hunger is winning" > "Tired of being hungry?")

---

## Dimension 2: Voice Authenticity (25 points)

Does it sound like the brand founder, not ChatGPT?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 25 | Distinctive voice. Could only be this brand. Uses founder's speech patterns, references, and perspective. |
| 20 | Good voice with minor generic moments. 90%+ feels authentic. |
| 15 | Mostly authentic but noticeable AI patterns (generic transitions, filler phrases). |
| 10 | Generic marketing copy. Could be any supplement/health brand. |
| 5 | Obvious AI-generated. Multiple generic phrases, exclamation marks, emoji clusters. |
| 0 | No text content. |

**Red flags (automatic deductions):**
- "In today's fast-paced world" (-10)
- "Let's dive in" (-5)
- "Game-changer" or "revolutionary" (-8)
- 3+ exclamation marks in a single post (-5)
- Emoji every other sentence (-5)

---

## Dimension 3: Value Density (25 points)

Does every sentence teach something or solve a problem?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 25 | Every sentence either teaches, explains a mechanism, or moves the reader closer to solving their problem. Zero filler. |
| 20 | Strong educational content with 1-2 transitional/filler sentences. |
| 15 | Good content mixed with engagement bait ("tag a friend", "drop a comment"). |
| 10 | More filler than substance. Generic advice ("eat clean, train hard"). |
| 5 | Pure engagement bait or promotional fluff. |
| 0 | No text content. |

**Filler patterns to flag:**
- "In this post, I'm going to..."
- "Stay tuned for more"
- "Follow for daily tips"
- "Share this with someone who..."
- "What do you think? Comment below"

---

## Dimension 4: Compliance + Platform Fit (25 points)

Correct character limits, no banned phrases, platform-native formatting.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 25 | Within all limits, zero banned phrases, feels native to the platform, includes appropriate hashtags/CTAs. |
| 20 | Minor formatting issue (1-2 chars over limit, or slightly off for platform). |
| 15 | One significant issue (banned phrase, wrong media format, missing hashtags on IG). |
| 10 | Multiple compliance issues. |
| 5 | Over character limit AND banned phrases present. |
| 0 | Would trigger regulatory or platform policy violation. |

**Hard failures (automatic 0 for this dimension):**
- FDA-noncompliant health claims
- Unsubstantiated disease treatment claims
- Platform-banned content (before/after for supplements)

---

## Scoring Process

1. Score each dimension independently (0-25)
2. Sum all four dimensions for total (0-100)
3. Apply verdict:
   - **PASS** (90-100): Ready for review dashboard
   - **WARN** (70-89): Needs minor revision, flag specific issues
   - **FAIL** (<70): Needs significant revision, provide rewrite guidance
4. If WARN or FAIL, provide specific feedback per dimension and re-generate
5. Max 3 iterations — if still below 90 after 3 rounds, flag for human review
